Understanding Massachusetts Social Service Terms, Made Simple.

The Massachusetts Social Service Word Key glossary is a free online tool that explains words used by the Department of Transitional Assistance in simple, plain language.

    Browse All Terms

    Why This Glossary Exists

    Official documents can be filled with confusing language. We believe that everyone deserves to understand the words that impact their rights. This tool was built to bridge the gap between complex systems and the communities they serve, empowering you to navigate the process with confidence.

    Does This Look Familiar?

    A letter from the DTA can be intimidating. Complex terms and unclear instructions can leave you feeling stressed and unsure of what to do next. You are not alone. This glossary is designed to turn that confusion into confidence by explaining every term in simple, easy-to-understand language.

            About the MSS Word Key

            Navigating Massachusetts social services can be challenging. Official notices and applications often use specific terminology that can be confusing, creating barriers for residents seeking essential support.

            The Massachusetts Social Services Word Key was created to solve this problem. It is a free, user-friendly online glossary designed to explain common DTA and SNAP terms in simple, plain language. Our goal is to empower all Massachusetts residents with the clarity and confidence needed to manage their benefits effectively.

            This website was developed as part of a Master's thesis project in Design and Digital Media at the University of Edinburgh, with a focus on improving access to information through user-centered design. We believe that everyone deserves to understand the words that impact their rights and well-being.

            How to Use This Glossary

            Welcome! This guide will help you get the most out of the MSS Word Key.

            Search for a Term

            The easiest way to find a definition is to use the search bar at the top of every page. Type the word you are looking for and results will appear as you type.

            Browse All Terms

            If you aren't sure of the exact term or want to explore, click the "Browse Terms" link in the menu. On that page, click any letter to see all glossary terms that start with that letter.

            Understand the Details

            Each term has its own page with a simple definition, an example sentence, an explanation of why it matters, and links to related terms and official resources.

            Designed for Everyone

            This site is built with accessibility in mind, featuring clear text, keyboard navigation, and language translation options to ensure everyone can get the information they need.
